About Me

Nothing excites an engineer more than witnessing the theoretical concepts taught in class come to life in an inventive industrial setting. Industrial engineering is a fascinating amalgamation of concepts such as sequencing, inventory management, optimization, and probability. My prior work experience has exposed me to the dynamics of manufacturing, quality, supply, safety, maintenance, and how it applies to the varied industrial processes, it has also made me realize the gaps in my skillsets. To succeed in the industrial realm, one needs a have a thorough understanding of manufacturing and service processes along with expertise in resource allocation, planning, quality assurance, inventory control while ensuring the safety of the individuals involved. To equip myself with the desired skillsets, I am pursuing a Masters in Industrial and Systems Engineering at Rutgers University. Bachelors in Mechanical Engineering familiarised me with various concepts of design, research, simulations, and also exposed me to manufacturing processes and Industrial engineering. My work has led me to work on global standards such as kanban, 5s, 6 sigma, kaizen, and TPM. Manufacturing techniques like requirements, planning, shift scheduling, controlling rejections were exhibited by me at my internships. Industry quality standards its management, nondestructive testing such as radiographic testing, liquid penetrant testing, magnetic particle testing are some of my key skills. Along with these safety measures such as HIRA, CAPA, and IMS certifications of ISO and OHSAS are among the work standards I have worked. I realized the importance of Industrial engineering and decided to pursue my career to partake in the Fourth industrial revolution.
