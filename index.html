About Me

Nothing excites an engineer more than witnessing the theoretical concepts taught in class come to life in an inventive industrial setting. Operations Research (OR) is a fascinating amalgamation of concepts such as sequencing, inventory management, optimization, and probability. The golden opportunity to see them translate into action presented itself during my work at Interio, a sheet-metal manufacturing giant that comprises of 35,000 SKUs. Working here in the capacity of a graduate engineer trainee, I am entrusted with the responsibility of planning and sequencing of products utilized in the manufacturing unit. Working on these bulky and random orders necessitates critical sequencing and scheduling. I am required to execute backward integration and ensure availability of man, machine, and material in the most optimal manner. While this experience has exposed me to the dynamics of Operations Research and how it applies to the varied industrial processes, it has also made me realize the gaps in my skillsets. To succeed in the operations realm, one needs a have a thorough understanding of manufacturing and service processes along with expertise in resource allocation, planning, quality assurance, inventory control while ensuring the safety of the individuals involved. To equip myself with the desired skillsets, I wish to pursue a Masters in Industrial Engineering with a focus on Operations Research from-----university.
